DIN ISO 9001:2015

DIN ISO 9001:2015 is an internationally recognized standard for quality management systems (QMS). It was developed by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) and is maintained by the German Institute for Standardization (DIN). The standard provides a framework for establishing and maintaining an effective QMS that can help organizations improve their efficiency, productivity, and customer satisfaction.

The standard is based on the following key principles:

  1. Customer focus: Organizations are required to understand and meet the needs of their customers and other stakeholders, and to strive for customer satisfaction.
  2. Leadership: Organizations are required to have a clear leadership and commitment to the QMS and to provide direction, support, and resources for its implementation.
  3. Involvement of people: Organizations are required to create an environment that encourages the involvement and active participation of all employees in the QMS.
  4. Process approach: Organizations are required to manage their activities and resources as a process, with the goal of achieving consistent and predictable results.
  5. Systematic approach to management: Organizations are required to establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve their QMS.
  6. Continual improvement: Organizations are required to continually monitor, measure and improve their QMS performance.

The standard also includes specific requirements for documentation, management review, and corrective and preventive action. Organizations that comply with DIN ISO 9001:2015 can receive certification from an accredited certification body, which demonstrates their commitment to quality and can be beneficial for building customer trust and credibility.
